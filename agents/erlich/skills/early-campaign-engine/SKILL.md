## Phase 1: Campaign Intake Interview

**Goal:** Understand exactly what this campaign is about before doing any research.

### Questions to Ask the Boss (All of These)

Ask these in a conversational, Erlich-style way ‚Äî not as a form. Group related questions naturally.

**Campaign Basics:**
1. What's the specific goal of this campaign? (e.g., get builders to integrate Early.build, launch awareness, drive signups, build an advocate program)
2. What's the target audience? (e.g., AI agent builders, DeFi devs, vibecoders, hackathon participants, Indian web3 builders, Base ecosystem, Solana devs ‚Äî be specific)
3. What's the timeline? (e.g., 2-week sprint, 8-week campaign, ongoing)
4. What's the budget range, if any? (flat fees per creator, bounties, rev share, tokens, or just "love and exposure")

**Product Context:**
5. Which Early.build features or value props should this campaign highlight? (e.g., dev tools discovery, trusted/maintained tools, community curation)
6. Is there a specific product launch, feature, or event tied to this campaign?
7. What assets exist already? (landing page, demo video, docs, pitch deck, sample content)

**Builder Criteria:**
8. What kind of builders are we looking for? (content creators, shipping builders, educators, DevRel, agents builders ‚Äî or all?)
9. Any specific chains/ecosystems to focus on? (Base, Solana, Monad, Scroll, chain-agnostic?)
10. Minimum follower/engagement bar? (or do we want a mix of big accounts + rising micro-builders?)
11. Any builders you already know or want included? (names/handles to seed the list)
12. Any builders to explicitly avoid?

**Success Definition:**
13. What does success look like? (e.g., "50 builders try the product", "10 production integrations", "100K impressions", "5 threads that go viral")
14. What's the #1 priority? (awareness vs signups vs production integrations vs community growth)
15. What are the biggest bottlenecks right now? (e.g., "nobody knows we exist", "builders try it but don't integrate", "can't reach the right people")

### After the Interview

Summarize what you heard back to the boss in this format:

```
## Campaign Brief: [Campaign Name]

**Goal:** [1 sentence]
**Target Audience:** [Specific builders/creators]
**Timeline:** [Duration]
**Budget:** [Range or "TBD"]
**Key Product Angle:** [What to highlight]
**Success Metric:** [The #1 number that matters]
**Priority:** [Awareness / Signups / Integrations / Community]
**Bottleneck:** [What's blocking us]
**Builder Criteria:** [Who we're looking for]
**Chains/Ecosystems:** [Focus areas]
**Existing Assets:** [What we have to work with]
**Seed List:** [Any known builders to include]
```

## Phase 2: Builder Discovery & Twitter Analysis

**Goal:** Find and analyze relevant builders/creators for this campaign using X/Twitter research.

### Step 1: Find Builders

Use your x-research tools and @bighead's data capabilities to find builders matching the campaign criteria.

**Search strategies:**
- Search X for keywords related to the campaign (e.g., "building on Base", "AI agents", "dev tools", "shipped this weekend")
- Look at who's engaging with competitor/similar products
- Check hackathon winners, DevRel accounts, ecosystem grant recipients
- Look at who the boss's seed list follows and engages with
- Check trending builders in the target ecosystem's Discord/Farcaster

**Target: Find 15-30 potential builders** ‚Äî mix of:
- 5-8 Tier A (high engagement, clear niche, 5K+ followers)

## Phase 3: Builder-to-Campaign Mapping & Outreach Plan

**Goal:** Map approved builders to the campaign with specific recommendations for content, outreach, and expected outcomes.

### Step 1: Quick Project Summary

Recap the campaign brief in 3-5 bullets (goals, priorities, bottlenecks, timing).

### Step 2: Recommended Split

- **Long-term builds & advocates (6-12+ weeks):** Production integrations, recurring mentions, case studies

### Step 4: Campaign Structure (8-12 Week Sprint)

Produce a structured campaign plan:

**Budget Split Estimate:**
- X% on Tier 1 creators (highest reach)
- Y% on Tier 2 builders (highest conversion)
- Z% on bounties/community (scale)

**Key Content Formats:**
- Agent of the Week ‚Äî Feature a builder who integrated Early.build tools
- Vibe & Build ‚Äî Quick "I built this with [tool from Early.build]" demos
- Built This Weekend ‚Äî Weekend hackathon-style content showing tool discovery ‚Üí build
- Tool Teardown ‚Äî In-depth reviews/comparisons of tools on Early.build
- Builder Spotlight Threads ‚Äî Profiles of builders using Early.build tools

**Bounty Ideas + Incentives:**
- Badge system (e.g., "Early Builder" badge for first integration)
- Revenue share for builders who drive signups
- Hall of fame / leaderboard for top contributors
- Exclusive early access to new features
- Cash bounties for production integrations

**Assets to Provide Creators:**
- Product demo video / GIF
- Key talking points (3-5 bullets)
- Landing page link + UTM parameters
- Sample thread/post copy they can adapt
- Screenshots / design assets
- Access to product (if gated)

**Tracking:**
- UTM links per creator (track signups by source)
- Engagement leaderboard (likes, replies, bookmarks per creator post)
- Production integration tracking (did the builder actually ship something?)
- Weekly reporting to boss

### Step 5: Why This Selection

Tie the recommendations back to the campaign priorities. Explain:
- Why these specific builders match the campaign goals
- Expected total reach / engagement
- Realistic conversion expectations
- What success looks like at the 4-week and 8-week marks

### Selection Rules (Follow These)

- **Prioritize relevance:** Match creator niche/content style to Early.build's use case (dev tools discovery, trusted tools, builder community)
- **Focus on production outcomes:** Signups are vanity. Prioritize creators who ship demos, write threads that lead to integrations
- **Be realistic on cost/engagement:** High-follower accounts = expensive but big reach. Mid-tier builders = higher conversion per dollar
- **Suggest low-friction starts:** Quick threads/videos first, then escalate to bounties/series
- **Skip mismatches:** Pure shitposters (unless viral potential), security-focused accounts (unless security tools angle), low-activity accounts (unless cheap and high-signal)
- **Mix tiers:** Don't go all Tier A. The sweet spot is 2-3 Tier A for reach + 4-6 Tier B for conversion + 2-4 Tier C for authenticity

## Phase 4: Outreach Execution

**After boss approves the mapping and campaign plan:**

1. **Research** ‚Äî Use the analysis from Phase 2 (you already have their content strengths, engagement patterns, audience)
2. **Hook** ‚Äî Reference something specific they recently posted
3. **Value proposition** ‚Äî Lead with what THEY get (exposure, tools, community, revenue share)
4. **CTA** ‚Äî Soft ask appropriate to the relationship stage
5. **Follow-up cadence** ‚Äî Plan the follow-up sequence

