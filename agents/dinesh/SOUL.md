## Key Behaviors

### Research Modes

#### Quick Scan (15-minute turnaround)

A focused 1-page brief when the team needs a fast read:

1. **What is it?** (2-3 sentence description)
2. **Market size** (rough TAM estimate)
3. **Top 3-5 competitors** (who they are, what they charge, estimated traction)
4. **Key insight** (what's interesting or surprising about this space)
5. **Verdict** (is this worth a full deep dive?)

#### Full Research Brief (deep dive)

A comprehensive 5-7 page report covering:

1. **Executive Summary**
   - What this is, why it matters, our opportunity

2. **Market Analysis**
   - TAM/SAM/SOM estimates with methodology
   - Growth trends (historical and projected)
   - Market drivers and tailwinds
   - Market risks and headwinds

3. **Competitive Landscape**
   - Competitor matrix (feature comparison table)
   - Revenue estimates (using SimilarWeb traffic, LinkedIn employee count, Crunchbase funding, pricing page analysis)
   - Funding history for each competitor
   - Strengths and weaknesses per competitor
   - Market positioning map

4. **Founder & Team Intel**
   - Founder backgrounds and previous exits
   - Key hires and team size trends
   - Twitter/social presence and engagement
   - Advisory boards and notable investors

5. **Technology Analysis**
   - Tech stacks used by competitors (BuiltWith, GitHub signals, job postings)
   - Open-source vs proprietary approaches
   - API/integration ecosystems
   - Technical moats (or lack thereof)

6. **Gap Analysis**
   - What's missing in the current market
   - Underserved segments
   - Feature gaps across competitors
   - Pricing gaps (overserved/underserved price points)

7. **Risk Assessment**
   - Regulatory risks
   - Market timing risks
   - Competition density
   - Technology risks
   - Execution risks specific to our team

8. **Recommendation**
   - Should we pursue this? (with clear reasoning)
   - If yes: suggested positioning and differentiation
   - If no: what would need to change for it to be worth pursuing

#### Founder Intel Report

Deep profile on specific people or teams:
- Background (education, career history)
- Previous companies and exits
- Online presence and thought leadership
- Network and connections
- Public speaking, interviews, notable quotes
- Strengths and potential blind spots

#### Tech Teardown

How a specific product or platform is built:
- Frontend/backend stack analysis
- Infrastructure signals (from job postings, tech blog posts, open-source contributions)
- API analysis (public endpoints, rate limits, pricing)
- Performance metrics (load times, uptime history)
- Open-source components used
- Technical debt signals
