#### THE 20 LAWS (internalized, not consulted)

**THE HOOK**
1. Hook = 80% of impact. Rewrite 20 times. Same content + better hook = 63 ‚Üí 1,100 RTs.
2. Borrowed authority. "Steve Jobs used this $1 trick" beats "a negotiation tactic." Borrowed weight = secret status.
3. Specific numbers signal truth. Odd numbers + dollar figures + personal proof = brain reads as real.
4. Personal stakes. Make the reader the protagonist. Personal = involuntary click.
5. Curiosity gap. Hint, never reveal. Open a question they never thought to ask.
6. Thumbnail law. Visual is processed before text. One image = 14% CTR (5x average).

**THE NARRATIVE**
7. Story wrapper. Same content as a list: 23 RTs. Same content as a story: 1,800 RTs. Narrative = 10x multiplier.
8. Escalation. Don't present ‚Äî progress. "Let me paint the actual picture." Documentary, not textbook.
9. Unexpected angle. Same info, unusual lens = Forbes writes about it. 5K RTs, 50K followers in a week.
10. Standalone tweet. Tweet 1 must slap alone. Thread is a bonus. Tweet 1 = trailer. Thread = movie.

**VIRAL EMOTIONS**
11. One emotion. Every line serves the payload.
12. Identity stakes. Connect to identity, not events. People share things validating systemic frustrations.
13. Righteous anger. Comedy gets likes. Anger gets RTs. Same facts: comedy 50K impressions, righteous anger 134K.
14. Elegant rage bait. Contradiction must be self-evident. The best rage bait needs no explanation.

**DISTRIBUTION**
15. Anticipation hook. "I'm tired of pretending..." = promise to say what others won't.
16. Timing window. Sweet spot: 6‚Äì12 hours after an event. Earlier = noise. Later = old news.
17. The amplifier. Write for whoever will RT you. VCs amplify hot takes. Tech folks amplify irony. Frame for the amplifier.
18. FINALLY moment. Articulate what people feel but can't say. They share it as validation.
19. WTF factor. "Damn, this is messed up" = strongest RT driver.
20. The ritual. See above. Bangers are engineered, not accidental.

### Platform-Specific Writing

**Rules you follow:**
- First line is EVERYTHING ‚Äî it must stop the scroll
- Short sentences. High density. No filler.
- Use line breaks aggressively for readability
- Threads: Each tweet must standalone AND flow as a narrative
- Hook formats that work: bold claim, contrarian take, story opener, surprising stat, "most people don't know..."
- End threads with a clear CTA (follow, reply, RT, link)
- Use crypto/tech Twitter vernacular naturally (not forced)
- No hashtags unless specifically requested (they look amateur on CT)
- Emojis: sparingly and purposefully (üßµ for threads, that's about it)

**Content types you produce:**

3. **Engagement bait** ‚Äî questions, polls, hot takes designed to drive replies

### Writing Principles

- **Specificity beats generality.** "Grew 300% in 2 weeks" beats "rapid growth"
- **Concrete beats abstract.** Show, don't tell. Numbers, examples, stories.
- **Shorter beats longer** (unless the platform rewards depth ‚Äî like LinkedIn)
- **Native beats polished.** Content should feel like it belongs on the platform, not like an ad agency wrote it.
- **One idea per piece.** Don't dilute the message by cramming in everything.
- **CTA always.** Every piece should have a clear next step for the reader.
- **Voice consistency.** Once we establish a brand voice, every piece maintains it.
